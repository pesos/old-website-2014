PES Summer of Code 2013

Hello friends!
Following up on PES Summer of Code 2012, we at PES Open Source Community wish to take a step further in our aim to make every PESITian a professional. So, we bring to you, PES Summer of Code 2.0!!

Who are we?
PES Open Source community is an initiative undertaken by the alumni and students of PESIT, Bangalore. It aims to popularize open source, come out with new hacks and projects and spread the word of being ‘open’.

Why such an excitement about ‘Open Source’?
Open-source software is software whose source code is published and made available to the public, enabling anyone to copy, modify and redistribute the source code. It is based on the philosophy that any form of knowledge must be shared. Monopoly on a software reduces its capability to evolve by making the entire user base’s productivity depend on the imagination and skills of just a handful of programmers.

What are these SoCs?
“Summer of Code” is a unique model of internship that was initiated by Google through its Google Summer of Code program. Many more SoCs like Summer of KDE (SoK), Ruby Summer of Code etc. are in place today.

Summer of Code programs are organized with the intention of introducing engineering students to the real world  product development and to draw more developers towards open source. These programs usually involve a student / a team of students working on certain products; usually on optimizing a product, or adding new features to it, sometimes, building new applications altogether. A list of ideas to work on is usually put up before application period begins to give the prospective applicants sufficient time to research on the ideas and brush up on their skills. New ideas can be put up by the applicants, and if found to be ‘awesome’, such ideas are readily accepted and a suitable mentor is assigned by the organizing team based on the description of the idea.

Each project in the ideas list will be associated with a mentor who will have a complete knowledge of the requirements for the project, and will have a clear idea on the nature of the end results. The mentor will help out the students under him by guiding them whenever and wherever possible, but won’t be doing the actual coding.

There will be a mid term evaluation sometime in the middle of the program where the mentors review participants’ progress and give a report on whether it’s worthwhile for the project to continue. The mentor is also evaluated based on whether he’s helpful, available for clarification of doubts etc.

What is PES Summer of Code?
PES SoC is a Summer of Code program initiated by the PES Open Source Community, to allow passionate students to work on interesting projects in their summer; projects that will benefit our college too, to make the administration of our college technically superior. The list of Project Ideas can be found here. A team of maximum three members can apply for a project (which may vary for individual projects). A team can apply for more than one project, but can be selected to only one. A single person can never be affiliated with two different selected teams. In such a case he/she would have to choose one of the teams, and the other team cannot ask for a replacement member. So, we would suggest you to be very careful when building your team.

If you are proposing your own idea, it must satisfy one of the two requirements

  1. It must be based on Open Source and / or
  2. It must have a real world application


 Most of the mentors will always be available in person; those who are not, will always be available online.

How to apply?
Go through the ideas list, and select a project based on your interests, skill sets etc. Research the idea thoroughly, discuss with the mentors to get a clear picture on what you are supposed to do, and prepare a proposal giving complete description of the project and its implementation details.  A proposal template can be found here. Send it to pesos@pes.edu and cc the mentor.

Timeline of the Programme

18th June Announcement of Ideas List
21st June, 12 PM End of application period
21st June, 9PM Announcement of selected proposals
22nd June Briefing session for selected teams
24th June Coding Period Starts
22nd July Mid Term Evaluation
19th August Final Evaluation
22nd August PES Summer of Code formal wind-up
