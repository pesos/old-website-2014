Ayana

Ayana 2012 is a unique event organized by the PES Open Source Community with the backing of The Department of Computer Science, PES Institute of Technology. It would consist of two parts – A string of Tech Talks by active Open Source Contributors and Hardware hackers from across India, and a 24 hour innovation fest ‘Hackathon’.

Tech Talks cover a wide range of topics from web technologies to mobile applications, hardware sessions to philosophical talks. A detailed list of our speakers and their topics can be found here

Hackathons generally command a strong crowd who stay within the campus and hack their way to glory in 24 hours. Its fun, exciting and stimulating. We hereby issue a call to action for students, developers and designers from all across India to come to PESIT, Bangalore and participate in this most awesome of events! Put on your programming shoes, grab that coffee mug and brace yourself for an exciting round of coding madness.

We will provide you with everything you need to have fun, be creative and productive, and impress your peers with what you can do with a computer and the wealth of technologies and data available on the Web.

Bogged down and clueless on what a ‘hack’ is or what you can develop? We have it sorted for you! We will be fueling your creativity with a variety of open source technologies and APIs, right from Android, Qt, Ruby to even kernel programming. We will keep you fed, watered and entertained for the duration of the event. All you need to bring is your creativity, your laptop, and your toothbrush


What is a Hackathon?

Many of you would have attended hackathons conducted by big wigs like Yahoo or Microsoft. But, what is hacking? For newbies to hacking here is a great introduction on Hacking 101 by Chris Heilmann or check out Ricky Montalvo’sHackumentary. The aim of this event is to promote hacker culture. A hacker is different from a network penetrator. To know the difference, read this article

Still not impressed? Know this — The Facebook like button was conceived as the result of an internal hack day!

Our event will begin with a series of hack-related presentations from some of the Industry’s most respected developers, startup gurus and open source geeks. We’ll then dive into 24 hours of hacking using a any collection of tools, services, APIs and libraries that YOU choose. We give you complete freedom on the choice of technologies that you would like to play around with. We do not restrict you to software hacks; hardware hacks are deeply admired. We will end the event on the second day with the hack demos plus bragging rights to the winner and give him a cash prize for his geekiness.

If you’d like to attend the event, then please register here. The number of participants is limited so we will be reviewing applications for attendance and giving preference to the ones who sign up first. You could be hacking individually or in groups but please sign-up individually.

 

What do you need to know?

Nothing! The ideal hackathon is one where you come to the place, learn something new and develop a nice, neat little hack/application using your newly acquired skills.

You need not be a programming freak or a Computer Science student to participate, Hackathons welcome students from various branches and sees ingenuous hardware hacks from the Electronics brethren as well.

 

Where and When?

The event will be held on the weekend of the 28th – 29th of April at the PESIT Campus, Bangalore

Website: http://pesit.pes.edu

Doors will open at 8:30 am on 28th April with the first program starting at 9:00am
